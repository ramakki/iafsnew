Travel to India

Airports & Airlines

India has five main gateways for international flights; however, a number of other cities such as Goa, Kochi (Cochin), Lucknow and Trivandrum also service international carriers.

The Major international airports are:
1) Indira Gandhi International Airport, New Delhi: www.newdelhiairport.in
2) Chattrapathi Shivaji International Airport, Mumbai: www.csia.in/
3) Bengaluru International Airport, Bengaluru: www.bengaluruairport.com
4) Rajiv Gandhi International Airport, Hyderabad: www.hyderabad.aero
5) Chennai International Airport, Chennai: http://www.aai.aero/chennai/index.jsp
Click here for a list of international airports: http://www.aai.aero/allAirports/airports.jsp

India's national carrier is Air India, which operates international and domestic flights.

Air Tickets

Departure tax and other charges are included in airline tickets. You are required to show a copy of your ticket and your passport in order to enter the airport, whether flying internationally or within India.

City Guide - Delhi

Delhi is India's capital city and the home of executive, legislative, and judiciary branches Government of India. Delhi is a large metropolis with strengths in arts, commerce, education, entertainment, fashion, finance, healthcare, media, professional services, research and development, tourism and transport all contributing to its prominence.

Districts of Delhi

South Delhi — Defence Colony, Hauz Khas, Green Park, Greater Kailash, Vasant Kunj, Lajpat Nagar, Nehru Place, Malviya Nagar and Kalkaji.
East Delhi — Gandhi Nagar, Preet Vihar, and Vivek Vihar.
North Delhi — Sadar Bazar, University Enclave (Kamla Nagar), Kotwali, and Civil Lines.
West Delhi — Patel Nagar, Rajouri Garden, East Sagarpur and Punjabi Bagh.
Central Delhi — Connaught Place, Khan Market, Chanakyapuri, Karol Bagh and Paharganj.
Old Delhi — Daryaganj, Kashmere Gate, Chandni Chowk, Chawri Bazaar, Lal Quila and Jama Masjid.

Weather

Best time to visit to Delhi is between October and March. October marks the onset of autumn. During these time you can experience very dry ambiance, warm days and pleasant nights. Maximum temperatures drop below 30°C by late October and there is a gradual fall in average temperature. Minimum temperature drops below 20°C.

Shopping

Delhi has a great mix of modern and traditional shopping. From sprawling malls to old bazaars, Delhi has it all.

Shopping

  • DLF Emporio, 4 Nelson Mandela Road, Vasant Kunj
  • DLF Promenade, 3 Nelson Mandela Road, Vasant Kunj
  • Ambience Mall, (Near J. Nehru University
  • Select City Walk, MGF Metropolitan, & DLF Place Malls, A-3m District Centre, Saket
  • Pacific Mall, Najafgarh Road, Tagore Garden, New Delhi

Bazaars

  • Connaught Place: A unique cross between a European shopping arcade, an Indian bazaar, and an upmarket shopping mall.
  • Paharganj, (opposite New Delhi Railway Station): This strip of shops sells items such as Indian perfumes, shawls, music instruments, rugs, jewellery, etc.
  • Rajouri Garden Market - Excellent place to shop for wedding as well as everyday clothing.
  • Khan Market: An up market shopping spot with a mix of restaurants, garment stores, housewares, jewellery/accessories, and books.
  • Janpath: Flea Market
  • Karol Bagh: traditional Indian Wear, sarees and shawls.
  • Chandni Chowk: The heart of Old Delhi, this is the place to go for the full-on Indian experience of crowded, twisting alleys and tiny shops.
  • Dilli Haat: Handicrafts

Sightseeing

  • Jama Masjid: India's largest mosque and one of Shah Jahan's masterpieces.
  • Qutub Minar: World's tallest brick minaret and an excellent example of Afghan Architecture.
  • Humayun's Tomb: Built by Humayun's widow, Queen Haji Begum in the 16th century.
  • Red Fort: Built by Shahjahan, The construction lasted from 1639 to 1648.
  • Akshardham: It is hailed as an eternal place of devotion, purity and peace.
  • India Gate: India's war memorial located astride the Rajpath, on the eastern edge of the 'ceremonial axis' of New Delhi.
  • Jantar Mantar: It's located in the modern city of New Delhi. It consists of 13 architectural astronomy instruments.

Eating Out

  • Indian Accent, Manor Hotel:  inspired modern Indian cuisine
  • Varq, Taj Mahal Hotel, New Delhi: North Indian and Seafood cuisines
  • Dakshin, Sheraton: a luxury South Indian restaurant
  • Bukhara, ITC Maurya: Bukhara is an unrivalled Global Culinary Destination.
  • Chandni Chowk: Variety of some of the best street food of Delhi
  • Wengers, Connaught Place: Is the old world charm of English baking and hosts the most delicious varieties of pastries, cakes, cookies & other confectionaries

Metro services / Routes and lines

  • Red Line: Connects Rithala in the west to Dilshad Garden in the east

  • Yellow Line: Connects Jahangirpuri with HUDA City Centre in Gurgaon

  • Blue Line: Connects Dwarka Sub City in the west with the satellite city of Noida in the east

  • Green Line: It runs between Inderlok (station on the Red Line) and Mundka with a branch line connecting the lines of Ashok Park Main station with Kirti Nagar station on the Blue Line

  • Violet Line: Connects Badarpur

  • Airport Express: The Airport Express line runs from New Delhi Railway Station to Dwarka Sector 21, via Indira Gandhi International Airport & Delhi Aerocity.
